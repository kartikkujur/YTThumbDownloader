Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it legal to download YouTube thumbnails?
Yes. Downloading thumbnails for personal use, research, or design inspiration is generally acceptable. However, reusing someone else's thumbnail for your own commercial content without permission may infringe their copyright. Always credit the original creator when in doubt.
Will I always get the 1280×720 HD version?
Only if the creator uploaded a custom HD thumbnail. Older videos or those without a custom thumbnail may not have a maxresdefault.jpg available — in that case, the HD slot may show an alternate quality. HQ and MQ versions are almost always available.
How does the 4K Ultra HD feature work?
Since YouTube stores high-res uploads up to 1080p/720p maximum, our system fetches the highest available master quality and processes it through a client-side vector grid matrix to scale it to uncompressed crisp 3840×2160 canvas resolutions for high-end editing software.
Does this work for YouTube Shorts?
Yes! YouTube Shorts also have thumbnail images associated with them. Paste the Shorts URL (e.g., youtube.com/shorts/...) and the tool will extract the thumbnail the same way.
Does it work on mobile (Android / iPhone)?
Absolutely. The tool is fully mobile-responsive. On mobile, after the image appears, long-press the image and select "Save Image" to download it to your gallery — no app needed.
